'10 Famous Monuments'

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan (reigned 1628–1658) to house the tomb of his favourite wife Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

  • Location: Agra, India
  • Built: 1632-1653
  • Style: Mughal
  • Materials: Marble, sandstone

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications that were built over centuries to protect the Chinese Empire from invaders. The walls wind their way from east to west across northern China, and are estimated to be over 13,000 miles long.

  • Location: Northern China
  • Built: 7th century BC to 17th century AD
  • Style: Fortifications
  • Materials: Stone, brick, earth

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an oval amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, Italy. Built of concrete and stone, it was the largest amphitheatre ever built, and is considered one of the greatest works of Roman engineering.

  • Location: Rome, Italy
  • Built: 70-80 AD
  • Style: Amphitheatre
  • Materials: Concrete, stone

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a pre-Columbian Mayan city located in the eastern Yucatán Peninsula, in present-day Mexico. The city was a major centre of the Mayan civilization from the 9th to the 12th centuries AD.

  • Location: Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico
  • Built: 600-1200 AD
  • Style: Mayan
  • Materials: Limestone, stucco

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca city located in the Cusco Region of Peru. The city was built on a mountain ridge high above the Urubamba River, and is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

  • Location: Cusco Region, Peru
  • Built: 1450-1456
  • Style: Inca
  • Materials: Stone, adobe
